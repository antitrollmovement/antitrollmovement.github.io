🔥 The Fire: From Pain to Purpose

The AntiTrollMovement wasn’t just born out of what I saw online. It was born out of what I lived.

I came out of a narcissistic relationship that held up a mirror to who I really am — someone deeply empathetic. For a long time, I thought that made me weak. I was told I was “too soft,” that I saw the good in people when I should’ve toughened up.

But through healing, I realised the truth: empathy isn’t weakness. It’s power.

The real weakness sits with the people who exploit it — trolls, bullies, manipulators. People who weaponise cruelty and call it “normal.” They thrive because our culture lets them. Because silence, excuses, and “that’s just the way it is” protect them.

But empathy flips that script. Empathy says: “I see your hate for what it is, and I won’t mirror it back. I’ll shine a light instead.”

This isn’t just about me. I know there are thousands of people with stories like mine — survivors of toxic relationships, abuse, or bullying — still struggling to make sense of it. Still wondering if they’re the problem.

I want them to know: you’re not weak. You’re empathetic. And empathy, with boundaries, is the strongest force we’ve got.

Knowledge is power. And when knowledge meets empathy, culture changes.

That’s what AntiTrollMovement stands for. That’s why I flipped my pain into purpose.
